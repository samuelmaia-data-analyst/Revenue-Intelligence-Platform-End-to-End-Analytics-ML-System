# ADR 0003: Streamlit Consumes Processed Artifacts Instead of Recomputing Core Logic

## Status

Accepted

## Context

Dashboards in portfolio projects often re-implement business logic, re-query raw inputs or recompute critical metrics on page load.

That creates two problems:

- hidden divergence from the official pipeline
- harder debugging when UI and batch disagree

## Decision

The Streamlit app reads processed artifacts and warehouse-aligned outputs generated by the batch pipeline.

It may filter, format and simulate scenario views, but it should not become an alternate orchestration center.

## Consequences

Positive:

- one source of business truth
- simpler smoke testing
- lower risk of UI-only regressions in critical calculations

Negative:

- dashboard freshness depends on batch execution
- some highly interactive use cases are intentionally out of scope

## Why This Is Worth It

For this repository, consistency and inspectability matter more than building a visually rich interface with hidden business logic duplication.
